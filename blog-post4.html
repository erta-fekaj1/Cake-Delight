The Science of Baking

Understanding the chemistry behind baking for perfect results every time.

1. The Role of Ingredients

Each ingredient in baking serves a specific function. Flour provides structure, sugar adds sweetness and moisture, eggs act as binders, and leavening agents help with rise.

2. The Chemistry of Leavening

Baking powder and baking soda react with acidic ingredients to release carbon dioxide, creating air pockets that make baked goods light and fluffy.

3. Temperature Matters

Oven temperature affects texture and consistency. Precise heat ensures even baking, proper rising, and avoids burnt or undercooked sections.

4. The Magic of Gluten

Gluten is formed when water mixes with flour, giving dough its elasticity. Kneading strengthens gluten, which affects the final texture of baked goods.

5. Caramelization and Maillard Reaction

Sugars in baked goods caramelize at high temperatures, creating a golden-brown crust. The Maillard reaction between proteins and sugars adds rich flavor.
